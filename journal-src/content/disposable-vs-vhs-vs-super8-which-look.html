Filmsera’s camera shelf isn’t a filter pack you slap on later — each Look is a live grade in the viewfinder. Pick wrong and the whole Film feels off: soft 8mm on a strobe-lit dance floor, hard disposable flash on a candlelit dinner. This is a practical picker for three families couples ask about most: disposable, VHS, and 8mm / Super 8. Browse them all in Look Lab before you Host.

Glossary for the night: the Host locks one Look for the Film; Guests shoot Shots; Frames stay sealed until Reveal; a Recap is how you rewatch the keepers.

Disposable — party flash and candid chaos

The disposable Look is high contrast, punchy color, and a hard little flash that turns a dark room into a scene. Backgrounds fall off. Faces pop. Blurry ceilings still somehow feel like a good night.

Reach for it when: reception energy, cocktail hour, after-party, anything with low light and high tolerance for grit. It’s the Look that makes a packed dance floor read as the night — not a poorly lit hotel ballroom.

Skip it when: soft ceremony portraits, golden-hour garden shots, or any moment where flash would feel rude. Disposable is honest about being a party camera.

If the night is about laughter you can hear from the kitchen, disposable is almost always the right Shelf pick.

VHS — dance-floor grit and soft degradation

VHS Looks lean into fuzzy, slightly degraded video haze — the texture of a camcorder tape someone found in a drawer. Contrast softens; edges smear; the Frame feels already remembered.

Reach for it when: late reception, DJ sets, neon, rain on windows, “we’re already in the montage” energy. It’s less about flattering skin and more about making the room feel like footage.

Skip it when: you want crisp Faces for grandparents, or a Recap that reads as timeless rather than era-coded. VHS is a mood piece; on a quiet vow exchange it can read as a gimmick.

8mm / Super 8 — soft home-movie grain

Super 8 and 8mm Looks bring soft grain, warm flicker, and the gentle softness of a home movie. Highlights bloom. Motion feels a little dreamy even in a still Shot.

Reach for it when: golden hour, getting ready, rehearsal dinner, morning-after brunch — the tender chapters around the loud ones. It’s the Look that makes a Film feel like something you’ll keep for decades.

Skip it when: the room is pitch dark and Guests need flash forgiveness. Soft grain doesn’t invent light; disposable’s flash does.

One Look per Film

Lock it before Guests join. Mid-reception Look shopping fractures the roll. If you want both soft vows and flash dance floor, Host two Films — or accept one family and lean in.

A fast picker for the night

  • Reception + after-party chaos → Disposable
  • Late dance floor / neon / “found footage” → VHS
  • Golden hour, getting ready, brunch → 8mm / Super 8
  • Still unsure → warm portrait / Instant families in Look Lab — safer on skin than hard VHS

Match the Look to the chapter you’re Hosting, not to every chapter of the weekend. A disposable Film for the reception and an 8mm Film for getting ready will beat one compromised Look for everything.

Test before you print the QR

The week before, Host a tiny Film with your wedding party. Shoot ten Shots under real lighting — string lights, a bathroom mirror, outdoor shade. Open at Reveal. If the Frames feel like the night you want, lock that Look. If not, swap once and retest.
